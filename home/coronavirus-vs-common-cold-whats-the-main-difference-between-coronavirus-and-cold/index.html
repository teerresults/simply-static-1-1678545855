So What’s Actual Difference between Coronavirus And Common Cold ?

Corona Virus Vs Common Cold 

First of all Common cold is a viral infection which is usually harmless. Its common among 6 years or younger children and also adults get infected 3-4 times a year. Common cold can occur through many viruses but rhinoviruses are the most common one.

The Coronavirus is also very contagious. It can spread through other infected people or animals also. Coronavirus or COVID-19 occurs through severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2  (SARS-CoV-2).  The Name of the diseases and virus is different here like common cold.

Corona Virus Symptoms 

The following symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure.*

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath

Common Cold Symptoms 

  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Slight body aches or a mild headache
  • Congestion
  • Sneezing
  • Generally feeling unwell (malaise)
  • Low-grade fever

Risk Factors of corona virus vs common cold

Corona Virus Risk Factors

For patients  who are admitted to the hospital and confirmed cases of the new coronavirus (COVID-19), the key risk factors for death are:

  • Advanced age
  • Signs of sepsis
  • Blood clotting issues

These risk factors come from a new retrospective study that evaluated data records for 191 patients—137 survivors and 54 non-survivors. Each patient had been admitted to one of two hospitals in Wuhan, China.

Common Cold Risk Factors

  • Age. Children younger than 6 are at greatest risk of colds, especially if they spend time in child-care settings.
  • Weakened immune system. Having a chronic illness or otherwise weakened immune system increases your risk.
  • Time of year. Both children and adults are more susceptible to colds in fall and winter, but you can get a cold anytime.
  • Smoking. You’re more likely to catch a cold and to have more-severe colds if you’re exposed to cigarette smoke.
  • Exposure. If you’re around many people, such as at school or on an airplane, you’re likely to be exposed to viruses that cause colds.
